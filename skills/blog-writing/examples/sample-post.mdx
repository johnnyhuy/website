This is the hook paragraph. It should be bold, direct, and under 100 words. Make a claim that establishes stakes. No setup, no "In this post I will..." — jump straight into the argument.

## First Claim-Driven Section

This section makes a specific claim and argues for it. Use short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph.

Support the claim with evidence, analysis, or examples. Use tables for comparisons:

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Old way of thinking | New way of thinking |
| Common mistake | Better approach |

End the section with a bold insight that connects back to the thesis.

## Second Claim-Driven Section

Continue building the argument. Each section should directly support the central thesis.

Use bullet lists for scannable takeaways:

- **Key insight one.** Brief elaboration of why this matters.
- **Key insight two.** Brief elaboration of why this matters.
- **Key insight three.** Brief elaboration of why this matters.

## Third Section With Framework

If the note includes a framework or model, present it clearly:

| Dimension | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Dimension 1 | What it means | Concrete example |
| Dimension 2 | What it means | Concrete example |
| Dimension 3 | What it means | Concrete example |

Explain why the framework matters and how to apply it.

## What's Next
